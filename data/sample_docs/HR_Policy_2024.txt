HR POLICIES AND PROCEDURES MANUAL
Company: Acme Enterprise Solutions
Version: 2024.1
Last Updated: January 1, 2024

SECTION 1: LEAVE POLICIES

1.1 MATERNITY LEAVE POLICY

Eligibility:
All permanent employees who have completed at least 12 months of continuous service are eligible for maternity leave benefits. Contract employees with a minimum contract duration of 18 months are also eligible.

Duration and Benefits:
Eligible permanent employees are entitled to 6 months (26 weeks) of maternity leave. The first 3 months are fully paid at the employee's regular base salary. The remaining 3 months are paid at 50% of the base salary.

How to Apply:
Employees must submit a maternity leave application at least 8 weeks before the expected delivery date. The form must be accompanied by a doctor's certificate confirming the pregnancy and expected due date. Submit the form to HR Department.

1.2 PATERNITY LEAVE POLICY

All permanent male employees are entitled to 15 days of paid paternity leave, to be taken within 3 months of the child's birth. Paternity leave cannot be split into multiple periods. Applications must be submitted to HR at least 2 weeks in advance.

1.3 ANNUAL LEAVE

All full-time employees receive 25 days of annual leave per calendar year. Leave must be approved by the direct line manager with at least 5 business days notice. Unused leave of up to 10 days may be carried over to the next year.

1.4 SICK LEAVE

Employees are entitled to 12 days of paid sick leave per year. For absences exceeding 3 consecutive days, a medical certificate is required. Sick leave does not carry over to the following year.

SECTION 2: COMPENSATION AND BENEFITS

2.1 SALARY STRUCTURE
The company follows a structured pay banding system with annual reviews in January. Salary increments are merit-based, typically ranging from 5% to 15% depending on performance ratings.

2.2 HEALTH INSURANCE
All permanent employees and their immediate family members (spouse and up to 2 children) receive comprehensive health insurance coverage from Day 1 of employment.

2.3 PROVIDENT FUND
Employer contributes 12% of the employee's basic salary to the Provident Fund. Employee contribution is also 12% of basic salary.

SECTION 3: CODE OF CONDUCT

3.1 WORKPLACE HARASSMENT POLICY
Acme Enterprise Solutions has a zero-tolerance policy for workplace harassment of any kind. All complaints must be reported to HR within 30 days of the incident. Anonymous reporting is available through the Ethics Hotline: 1800-ETHICS.

3.2 CONFIDENTIALITY AGREEMENT
All employees are required to sign and adhere to the confidentiality agreement. Sharing of proprietary information, client data, or trade secrets is strictly prohibited and may result in immediate termination and legal action.

3.3 SOCIAL MEDIA POLICY
Employees must not share confidential company information on social media platforms. Any company-related posts must be approved by the Communications department.

SECTION 4: GRIEVANCE PROCEDURE

Step 1: Discuss the issue with your direct manager within 5 business days.
Step 2: If unresolved, escalate to HR Business Partner within 10 business days.
Step 3: HR will investigate and respond within 15 business days.
Step 4: If still unresolved, escalate to the HR Director.
Step 5: Final escalation is to the CEO's office.

SECTION 5: REMOTE WORK POLICY

Employees may work remotely up to 3 days per week with manager approval. Core hours of 10 AM to 3 PM must be maintained across time zones. Remote work equipment (laptop, internet allowance of INR 1000/month) is provided by the company.
